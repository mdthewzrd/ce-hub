### Understanding Digital Root

Digital root is simple: you keep adding the digits of a number until you get a single digit.

**Example:**
- 16 → 1 + 6 = 7
- 128 → 1 + 2 + 8 = 11 → 1 + 1 = 2
- 9999 → 9 + 9 + 9 + 9 = 36 → 3 + 6 = 9

This isn't numerology—it's a recognized mathematical operation used in computer science and number theory. What VBM discovers is what happens when you apply this to doubling sequences.

## The Doubling Circuit: 1-2-4-8-7-5

Let's double the number 1:

**The Doubling Sequence:**
- 1 × 2 = 2
- 2 × 2 = 4
- 4 × 2 = 8
- 8 × 2 = 16 → 1 + 6 = **7**
- 7 × 2 = 14 → 1 + 4 = **5**
- 5 × 2 = 10 → 1 + 0 = **1**

We're back to 1! We've completed a cycle: **1 → 2 → 4 → 8 → 7 → 5 → 1**

This six-number sequence is called the **doubling circuit**, and it's one of the fundamental patterns of the universe.

### Where Doubling Appears in Nature

**Nuclear Reactions:**
- Chain reactions follow doubling: 1 → 2 → 4 → 8
- Geometric progression in energy release

**Binary Computing:**
- All computers operate on doubling: 1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32, 64...
- The binary code that powers our digital world

The doubling circuit is everywhere because it's how energy expands and propagates through reality.

## The Halving Circuit: Reverse Doubling

What happens if we go the other way—halving instead of doubling?

**The Halving Sequence:**
- 1 ÷ 2 = 0.5 → **5**
- 5 ÷ 2 = 2.5 → 2 + 5 = **7**
- 7 ÷ 2 = 3.5 → 3 + 5 = **8**
- 8 ÷ 2 = **4**
- 4 ÷ 2 = **2**
- 2 ÷ 2 = **1**

Same circuit, opposite direction: **1 ← 5 ← 7 ← 8 ← 4 ← 2 ← 1**

This is the **halving circuit**, and it demonstrates something profound:

> **When you have motion in one direction, you always have trailing motion in the opposite direction.**

This is why waves have crests and troughs. Why breathing has inhalation and exhalation. Why the heart has systole and diastole. Energy never moves in only one direction—it's always a back-and-forth oscillation.

### What is a Torus?

A torus is a donut-shaped surface—a sphere with a hole through the center. But it's not just any donut:

- Energy spirals **inward** at the top
- Moves through the center
- Spirals **outward** at the bottom
- Returns to the top to begin again

This **doubling circuit** spirals around the torus continuously, creating a self-sustaining energy flow.

The torus is the basic pattern that energy takes because it's the most efficient way for energy to move while maintaining its coherence.
