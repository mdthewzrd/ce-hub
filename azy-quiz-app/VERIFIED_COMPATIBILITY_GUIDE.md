## üéØ Core Styling Principle

> **"Complement, Don't Match"**
> Create CONTRAST between frame shape and face shape for balance.

- **Round faces** ‚Üí Angular frames (square, rectangle, wayfarer)
- **Angular faces** ‚Üí Rounded frames (round, oval, cat-eye)
- **Oval faces** ‚Üí Everything works (balanced proportions)

## üìñ Detailed Compatibility Rules

### 1. AVIATOR Frames
**Shape:** Teardrop, double bridge, often brow bar

**Best For:**
- ‚úÖ **OVAL** - Perfect match, any style works
- ‚úÖ **SQUARE** - Softens strong jawlines (choose rounded edges)
- ‚úÖ **ROUND** - Adds definition (choose wider frames)
- ‚úÖ **HEART** - Top-heavy design balances wider forehead

**Use With Caution:**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **DIAMOND** - Can work but may highlight cheekbones
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **TRIANGLE** - Can work if balanced properly

**Why It Works:**
- Teardrop shape creates softness for angular faces
- Brow bar adds structure/contrast for round faces
- Top-heavy design balances heart-shaped faces
- Versatile enough for multiple face shapes

**Sources:**
- [Zenni Optical Aviator Guide](https://www.zennioptical.com/blog/mens-aviator-sunglasses/)
- [Cent Magazine Style Guide](https://centmagazine.co.uk/best-face-shapes-for-aviator-glasses-a-complete-style-guide/)
- [Ray-Ban Face Shape Guide](https://www.ray-ban.com/usa/c/face-shape-guide)

### 2. CAT-EYE Frames
**Shape:** Upswept outer corners, often decorative

**Best For:**
- ‚úÖ **OVAL** - Most versatile, any cat-eye style works
- ‚úÖ **HEART** - Upswept corners balance wider forehead
- ‚úÖ **SQUARE** - Angular lines create contrast with jaw
- ‚úÖ **ROUND** - Adds angular definition and structure
- ‚úÖ **DIAMOND** - Complements high cheekbones

**Not Recommended:**
- ‚ùå **TRIANGLE** - Can emphasize narrow forehead

**Why It Works:**
- Upswept design lifts and balances face proportions
- Angular lines create contrast for soft/round features
- Highlights cheekbones on diamond faces
- Bottom-heavy shape balances heart-shaped faces
- Works for almost every face shape when styled right

## üé® Summary by Face Shape

### OVAL Face
**Most versatile - can wear any frame style**

**Recommended:** All 6 styles work well
**Best choices:** Experiment with different styles
**Avoid:** None - you're lucky!

---

### ROUND Face
**Needs angular definition**

**Best choices:**
- Square frames (angular contrast)
- Rectangle frames (adds structure)
- Wayfarer frames (angular definition)
- Cat-eye frames (adds structure)

**Use with caution:**
- Aviator frames (choose wider for definition)

**Avoid:**
- Round frames (emphasizes roundness)

---

### SQUARE Face
**Needs softness and curves**

**Best choices:**
- Round frames (softens angles)
- Cat-eye frames (creates contrast)
- Aviator frames (softens jawline)

**Avoid:**
- Square frames (emphasizes angularity)
- Rectangle frames (too angular)
- Wayfarer frames (competing angles)

---

### HEART Face
**Needs bottom-heavy balance**

**Best choices:**
- Cat-eye frames (balances forehead)
- Aviator frames (top-heavy design)
- Round frames (adds softness)
- Square frames (adds structure)
- Rectangle frames (adds width to lower face)
- Wayfarer frames (balances proportions)

**Avoid:**
- Triangle face shape concerns (none for heart)

---

### DIAMOND Face
**Needs to soften cheekbones**

**Best choices:**
- Cat-eye frames (complements cheekbones)
- Oval frames (similar to round)

**Use with caution:**
- Aviator frames (may highlight cheekbones)
- Round frames (choose carefully)
- Wayfarer frames (proper proportions)

**Avoid:**
- Square frames (too geometric)
- Rectangle frames (highlights cheekbones)

---

### TRIANGLE Face
**Needs top-heavy width**

**Best choices:**
- Wayfarer frames (adds forehead width)
- Cat-eye frames (can work with right style)

**Use with caution:**
- Aviator frames (can work if balanced)
- Round frames (depends on proportions)

**Avoid:**
- Rectangle frames (bottom-heavy doesn't balance)
- Square frames (doesn't complement shape)
